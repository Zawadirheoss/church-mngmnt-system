Gospel Springs Mission

Church Bylaws

Founded in 1996 by Bishop William Laktari, Gospel Springs Mission began as a small gathering of believers in Isongo, Kakamega County. These bylaws, first adopted in 1998, establish the operational guidelines for our church's governance and ministry.

Article I: Membership

Membership in Gospel Springs Mission is open to all who profess faith in Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior, have been baptized by immersion, and agree to abide by the church covenant.

Section 1: Requirements

To become a member, an individual must:

  1. Make a public profession of faith in Jesus Christ
  2. Be baptized by immersion after conversion
  3. Complete the membership orientation course
  4. Affirm agreement with the church's statement of faith and covenant
  5. Be approved by the elders

Section 2: Responsibilities

Members are expected to:

  • Attend worship services regularly
  • Participate in the life and ministry of the church
  • Give financially as the Lord prospers
  • Submit to the spiritual leadership of the church
  • Live in accordance with biblical standards

Section 3: Termination

Membership may be terminated by:

  1. Request of the member
  2. Death
  3. Transfer to another church
  4. Church discipline (Matthew 18:15-17)

Article II: Church Officers

The officers of the church shall be pastors/elders and deacons, whose qualifications and responsibilities are defined in Scripture (1 Timothy 3:1-13; Titus 1:5-9; 1 Peter 5:1-4).

Section 1: Pastors/Elders

The pastors/elders are responsible for:

  1. Spiritual oversight of the congregation
  2. Teaching and preaching God's Word
  3. Shepherding and caring for members
  4. Providing overall direction for the church
  5. Administering the ordinances

Section 2: Deacons

The deacons assist the elders by:

  1. Serving practical needs of the congregation
  2. Managing church resources and facilities
  3. Facilitating ministry operations
  4. Supporting the elders in pastoral care

Section 3: Selection Process

Officers shall be selected as follows:

  1. Potential candidates shall be identified by the existing leadership
  2. Candidates shall be examined for biblical qualifications
  3. The congregation shall be given opportunity to affirm the candidates
  4. New officers shall be installed through prayer and laying on of hands

Article III: Meetings

The church shall hold regular worship services on Sundays and may hold other meetings as needed for worship, prayer, fellowship, and business.

Section 1: Worship Services

The church shall gather for worship each Sunday, with the following elements:

  • Prayer
  • Singing
  • Scripture reading
  • Preaching of God's Word
  • Ordinances as scheduled

Section 2: Business Meetings

An annual business meeting shall be held to:

  1. Review the previous year's ministry
  2. Present and approve the annual budget
  3. Elect officers as needed
  4. Address other important matters

Section 3: Special Meetings

Special business meetings may be called by the elders with at least two weeks' notice, except in emergencies.

Section 4: Quorum

For official business, a quorum shall consist of at least one-third of the voting membership.

Section 5: Voting

All members in good standing may vote. Decisions shall be made by majority vote unless otherwise specified in these bylaws.

Article IV: Amendments

These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of members present at a duly called business meeting, provided the amendment has been announced at least two weeks in advance.

Section 1: Proposal

Amendments may be proposed by:

  1. The elders
  2. A written petition signed by at least 20% of voting members

Section 2: Process

The amendment process shall include:

  1. Written notice to all members at least two weeks prior to the vote
  2. Opportunity for discussion at a specially called meeting
  3. Two-thirds majority vote of members present for adoption
