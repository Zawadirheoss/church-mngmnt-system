Leadership Code

Date of Adoption: 1998

Last Amended: 1998

All leaders of Gospel Springs Mission shall meet the biblical qualifications for their office and commit to the following standards of conduct and ministry philosophy.

I. Biblical Qualifications

1. Elders/Pastors (1 Timothy 3:1-7; Titus 1:5-9)
  1. Above reproach in character and reputation
  2. Husband of one wife (if married)
  3. Temperate, self-controlled, and respectable
  4. Hospitable and able to teach
  5. Not given to drunkenness or violence
  6. Gentle, not quarrelsome
  7. Not a lover of money
  8. Manages his own household well
  9. Not a recent convert
  10. Good reputation with outsiders
2. Deacons (1 Timothy 3:8-13)
  1. Worthy of respect
  2. Sincere, not double-tongued
  3. Not indulging in much wine
  4. Not pursuing dishonest gain
  5. Holding to the faith with clear conscience
  6. Tested and proved blameless
  7. Husband of one wife (if married)
  8. Managing children and household well

II. Character Commitments

1. Spiritual Vitality

Leaders will maintain a vibrant personal relationship with Christ through regular Bible study, prayer, and spiritual disciplines.

John 15:5; Colossians 3:16
2. Moral Purity

Leaders will maintain sexual purity in thought, word, and deed, avoiding all forms of sexual immorality and inappropriate relationships.

1 Thessalonians 4:3-5; Ephesians 5:3
3. Financial Integrity

Leaders will manage personal and church finances with complete integrity, avoiding conflicts of interest and maintaining transparency.

Proverbs 11:1; Romans 12:17
4. Family Priorities

Leaders will give proper priority to family relationships, ensuring their ministry does not come at the expense of spouse or children (if applicable).

1 Timothy 3:4-5; Ephesians 5:25
5. Emotional Health

Leaders will cultivate emotional maturity, dealing with stress, anger, and conflict in biblical ways.

Proverbs 16:32; Ephesians 4:26-27

III. Ministry Standards

1. Servant Leadership

Leaders will follow Christ's example of servant leadership, avoiding authoritarianism or lording over those in their care.

Mark 10:42-45; 1 Peter 5:2-3
2. Team Ministry

Leaders will work cooperatively with other leaders, valuing plurality in decision-making and mutual accountability.

Proverbs 11:14; Ecclesiastes 4:9-12
3. Doctrinal Fidelity

Leaders will teach and model sound doctrine, refuting false teachings and protecting the church from error.

Titus 1:9; 2 Timothy 4:2-3
4. Pastoral Care

Leaders will shepherd God's flock with compassion, being available to counsel, pray for, and encourage members.

1 Peter 5:2-4; Acts 20:28
5. Continual Growth

Leaders will pursue ongoing spiritual, theological, and leadership development through study, training, and mentorship.

Proverbs 1:5; 2 Peter 3:18

IV. Accountability

1. Peer Accountability

Leaders submit to regular evaluation by fellow leaders regarding their ministry effectiveness and personal walk with Christ.

Galatians 6:1-2; James 5:16
2. Congregational Oversight

Leaders remain accountable to the congregation they serve, being open to loving correction when needed.

1 Timothy 5:19-20; Hebrews 13:17
3. Restoration Process

Leaders who fall into sin but demonstrate genuine repentance will be restored according to biblical principles, though some restrictions may apply.

Galatians 6:1; 2 Corinthians 2:5-8
4. Removal from Office

Leaders who persist in unrepentant sin, false teaching, or who no longer meet biblical qualifications must be removed from leadership.

1 Timothy 5:20; Titus 3:10-11
