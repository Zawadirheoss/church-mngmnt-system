Membership Policy

Date of Adoption: 1998

Last Amended: 1998

Membership in Gospel Springs Mission represents a covenant relationship between believers who agree to walk together in Christian fellowship under the lordship of Jesus Christ and the authority of Scripture.

I. Membership Requirements

1. Profession of Faith

Individuals must profess faith in Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior, demonstrating evidence of regeneration through changed life and attitudes.

Romans 10:9-10; 2 Corinthians 5:17
2. Believer's Baptism

Members must have been baptized by immersion as believers, in obedience to Christ's command, following their conversion.

Matthew 28:19; Acts 2:41
3. Orientation

Prospective members must complete the membership orientation classes covering our Statement of Faith, Church Covenant, and ministry philosophy.

4. Agreement

Members must affirm agreement with our Statement of Faith and commit to abide by the Church Covenant.

5. Elder Approval

Final approval for membership rests with the elders after personal interview and affirmation by the congregation.

II. Membership Responsibilities

1. Regular Attendance

Members commit to regular participation in Sunday worship services and other church gatherings unless providentially hindered.

Hebrews 10:24-25
2. Spiritual Growth

Members will pursue spiritual maturity through personal Bible study, prayer, and participation in discipleship opportunities.

2 Peter 3:18; Ephesians 4:15
3. Financial Support

Members commit to regular, proportionate giving as God prospers them, supporting the ministry and mission of the church.

1 Corinthians 16:2; 2 Corinthians 9:7
4. Service

Members will use their spiritual gifts to serve in the ministries of the church according to their abilities and opportunities.

1 Peter 4:10; Romans 12:6-8
5. Biblical Living

Members agree to live according to biblical standards, maintaining a good reputation both within and outside the church.

Colossians 1:10; 1 Thessalonians 4:1

III. Membership Termination

1. Voluntary Withdrawal

Members may request withdrawal from membership for personal reasons or when transferring to another church.

2. Automatic Removal

Membership terminates automatically upon death or prolonged (over one year) unexplained absence from church life.

3. Church Discipline

Members may be removed through church discipline for unrepentant sin or doctrinal deviation after following the process outlined in Matthew 18:15-17.

Matthew 18:15-17; 1 Corinthians 5:1-5
4. Restoration

Former members under discipline may be restored upon evidence of repentance and reconciliation.

2 Corinthians 2:6-8; Galatians 6:1

IV. Membership Privileges

1. Voting Rights

Members in good standing may vote in church business meetings on matters of budget, leadership selection, and other major decisions.

2. Leadership Eligibility

Only members may serve in teaching or leadership positions within the church.

3. Pastoral Care

Members receive priority in pastoral care, counseling, and support during life events (weddings, funerals, etc.).
