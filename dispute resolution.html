In accordance with Matthew 18:15-17 and 1 Corinthians 6:1-8, members of Gospel Springs Mission agree to resolve conflicts through the following biblical process:

I. Conflict Resolution Process

Step 1: Private Resolution (Matthew 18:15)

If a brother or sister sins against you:

  1. Go directly to the person privately
  2. Point out the fault between you two alone
  3. Seek reconciliation and forgiveness
"If your brother sins against you, go and tell him his fault, between you and him alone." - Matthew 18:15
Step 2: Mediated Resolution (Matthew 18:16)

If private resolution fails:

  1. Bring one or two spiritually mature witnesses
  2. Seek mediation and objective perspective
  3. Document the process if necessary
"But if he does not listen, take one or two others along with you..." - Matthew 18:16
Step 3: Church Involvement (Matthew 18:17)

If mediation fails:

  1. Bring the matter before the church elders
  2. Elders will investigate and make recommendation
  3. Church may need to exercise discipline
"If he refuses to listen to them, tell it to the church." - Matthew 18:17

II. Church Discipline Process

1. Purpose of Discipline
  • Restore the sinning member
  • Protect the church body
  • Maintain the church's witness
Galatians 6:1; 1 Corinthians 5:6-7
2. Levels of Discipline
  1. Private admonition (1 Thessalonians 5:14)
  2. Public rebuke (1 Timothy 5:20)
  3. Removal from membership (Matthew 18:17)
  4. Withdrawal of fellowship (2 Thessalonians 3:14-15)
3. Restoration Process

When repentance is evident:

  1. Private reconciliation with offended parties
  2. Public restoration before congregation
  3. Gradual restoration to fellowship
2 Corinthians 2:6-8; Galatians 6:1

III. Mediation Guidelines

1. Selection of Mediators
  • Mature believers not involved in the dispute
  • At least one elder for serious matters
  • Mutually agreed upon by both parties
2. Mediation Process
  1. Sign confidentiality agreement
  2. Present written statements of grievance
  3. Joint and separate meetings
  4. Written resolution agreement
3. Binding Arbitration

For unresolved disputes:

  • Three-person panel of elders
  • Final decision binding on both parties
  • No civil lawsuits permitted
1 Corinthians 6:1-8
