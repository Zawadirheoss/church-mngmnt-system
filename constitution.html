Gospel Springs Mission

Church Constitution

Founded in 1996 by Bishop William Laktari, Gospel Springs Mission began as a small gathering of believers in Isongo, Kakamega County. Through years of faithful ministry, the church has grown to become a beacon of hope in the community, dedicated to spreading the Gospel and serving those in need.

This constitution, first adopted in 1998, establishes our beliefs, governance, and practices as we continue our mission to make disciples of Jesus Christ.

Date of Adoption: 1998 | Last Amended: 1998

Full Constitution

We, the members of Gospel Springs Mission, in order to carry out more effectively the mission of our Lord Jesus Christ, do hereby adopt this constitution as our articles of governance, to be interpreted at all times to reflect the character of and bring glory to Jesus Christ, as revealed in the Holy Bible and articulated in the standards of our faith.

Article I: Name

The name of this organization shall be Gospel Springs Mission, located in Isongo, along Isongo Ingoste Road, Navakholo Subcounty, Kakamega County.

Article II: Purpose

The purpose of Gospel Springs Mission is to glorify God by:

  1. Worshipping Him in spirit and truth
  2. Proclaiming the gospel of Jesus Christ through evangelism and discipleship
  3. Building up believers through biblical teaching and fellowship
  4. Serving our community through acts of love and compassion
  5. Planting and supporting other like-minded churches

Article III: Affiliation

Gospel Springs Mission is an autonomous local church that may voluntarily associate with other churches, denominations, or networks that hold to similar biblical convictions.

Church Bylaws

Article I: Membership

Membership in Gospel Springs Mission is open to all who profess faith in Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior, have been baptized by immersion, and agree to abide by the church covenant.

Article II: Church Officers

The officers of the church shall be pastors/elders and deacons, whose qualifications and responsibilities are defined in Scripture (1 Timothy 3:1-13; Titus 1:5-9; 1 Peter 5:1-4).

Article III: Meetings

The church shall hold regular worship services on Sundays and may hold other meetings as needed for worship, prayer, fellowship, and business.

Article IV: Amendments

These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of members present at a duly called business meeting, provided the amendment has been announced at least two weeks in advance.

Statement of Faith

We believe in:

  1. The verbal inspiration and authority of the Scriptures (2 Timothy 3:16-17)
  2. The one true God existing eternally in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit (Matthew 28:19)
  3. The virgin birth, sinless life, atoning death, bodily resurrection, and imminent return of Jesus Christ (1 Corinthians 15:3-4)
  4. The total depravity of man and salvation by grace through faith in Christ alone (Ephesians 2:8-9)
  5. The present ministry of the Holy Spirit in regeneration, sanctification, and empowerment (John 16:8-11)
  6. The resurrection of both the saved and the lostâ€”the saved to eternal life and the lost to eternal punishment (Revelation 20:11-15)
  7. The spiritual unity of all believers in the Lord Jesus Christ (Ephesians 4:3-6)
  8. The local church as God's primary instrument for accomplishing His work in this age (Matthew 16:18)

Policies & Procedures

Worship Services

Our worship services shall be conducted in a manner that glorifies God, edifies believers, and communicates the gospel clearly. The preaching of God's Word shall be central to our gatherings.

Ordinances

We observe two ordinances: believer's baptism by immersion and the Lord's Supper, which shall be administered regularly as acts of obedience and remembrance.

Child Dedication

Parents may present their children before the church for dedication, committing to raise them in the nurture and admonition of the Lord.

Facility Use

The church facilities are to be used primarily for church activities. Outside groups may use the facilities with prior approval, provided their use aligns with our values.

Membership Policy

Membership in Gospel Springs Mission is open to all who:

  1. Profess faith in Jesus Christ as personal Lord and Savior
  2. Have been baptized by immersion as a believer
  3. Complete the membership orientation
  4. Agree to abide by the church covenant
  5. Are approved by the elders

Membership Responsibilities

Members are expected to:

  • Attend worship services regularly
  • Participate in the life and ministry of the church
  • Give financially as the Lord prospers
  • Submit to the spiritual leadership of the church
  • Live in accordance with biblical standards

Membership Termination

Membership may be terminated by:

  1. Request of the member
  2. Death
  3. Transfer to another church
  4. Church discipline (Matthew 18:15-17)

Leadership Code

All leaders of Gospel Springs Mission shall:

  1. Meet the biblical qualifications for their office (1 Timothy 3; Titus 1)
  2. Serve with humility, integrity, and diligence (1 Peter 5:1-4)
  3. Be accountable to one another and the congregation
  4. Make decisions prayerfully, seeking God's will through Scripture and the Holy Spirit
  5. Model Christian character in their personal, family, and professional lives
  6. Continually grow in their knowledge of God and His Word
  7. Refrain from any behavior that would bring reproach on Christ's church

Pastors/Elders

The pastors/elders are responsible for spiritual oversight, teaching, shepherding, and overall direction of the church.

Deacons

The deacons assist the elders by serving practical needs, managing resources, and facilitating ministry operations.

Dispute Resolution

In accordance with Matthew 18:15-17 and 1 Corinthians 6:1-8, members of Gospel Springs Mission agree to resolve conflicts according to the following principles:

  1. First attempt private reconciliation between the parties involved
  2. If unsuccessful, seek mediation with one or two spiritually mature members
  3. If still unresolved, bring the matter before the church elders for resolution
  4. Refrain from lawsuits against other members, choosing Christian mediation instead

Church Discipline

The purpose of church discipline is restoration, not punishment. The process may include private admonition, public rebuke, or removal from membership in cases of unrepentant sin.

Amendments

This constitution may be amended by the following process:

  1. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the elders
  2. The elders will review the proposal for biblical consistency
  3. If approved by the elders, the amendment will be presented to the congregation
  4. The amendment must be announced at two consecutive Sunday services
  5. A special business meeting will be called to vote on the amendment
  6. A two-thirds majority of members present is required for adoption

Finances

Gospel Springs Mission shall be supported through the tithes and offerings of its members and attendees. All funds shall be managed with integrity and transparency.

Financial Policies

  1. The annual budget shall be prepared by the finance team and approved by the elders
  2. All expenditures shall follow the approved budget or receive special approval
  3. Two authorized signers shall be required for all checks
  4. Financial records shall be reviewed annually by an independent party
  5. Detailed financial reports shall be presented to the congregation quarterly

Meetings

Regular Meetings

The church shall hold weekly worship services on Sunday mornings and may schedule other regular meetings for prayer, Bible study, and fellowship.

Business Meetings

An annual business meeting shall be held to:

  1. Review the previous year's ministry
  2. Present and approve the annual budget
  3. Elect officers as needed
  4. Address other important matters

Special Meetings

Special business meetings may be called by the elders with at least two weeks' notice, except in emergencies.

Quorum

For official business, a quorum shall consist of at least one-third of the voting membership.
